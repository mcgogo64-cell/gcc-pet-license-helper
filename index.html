Start Process

Process Map

The process of bringing your pet to GCC countries can seem complex. We've broken it down into 6 main steps. Click the titles for details.

Step 1: Planning (6+ Months Out)

+

Timing: Start the process at least 6 months in advance.

Relocator: Research pet-friendly airlines and cargo services (e.g., Emirates SkyCargo, Qatar Airways Cargo).

Rule Check: Check the most current import rules for your destination country (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, etc.).

Step 2: Veterinary Prep

+

Microchip: A 15-digit, ISO 11784/11785 compliant microchip is mandatory. All procedures must be done *after* this chip is implanted.

Rabies Vaccine: A valid rabies vaccine is required. Usually done within the last 1 year and at least 21 days must have passed.

Other Vaccines: Full DHPPL (for dogs) or FVRCP (for cats) vaccinations are needed.

Step 3: Rabies Titer Test

+

Requirement: Mandatory for pets coming from "high-risk" countries (many countries, including Turkey, are on this list).

Timing: A blood sample must be taken at least 30 days *after* the rabies vaccination.

Validity: The test must be done at least 3 months (90 days) before arrival. Getting results (≥0.5 IU/ml) can take weeks.

Step 4: Permits & Documents

+

Import Permit: Must be obtained from the destination country's relevant ministry (e.g., MOCCAE for UAE, MEWA for Saudi Arabia). This permit is often valid for 30 days.

Health Certificate: An official, government-endorsed health certificate is needed just before the flight (usually within the last 10 days).

Step 5: Shipping & Flight

+

Cargo: Most GCC countries require pets to arrive as manifested cargo. They may not be accepted in-cabin or as excess baggage.

Travel Crate: An IATA-approved, properly sized, and secure travel crate is mandatory.

Booking: Book the flight immediately after your import permit is approved.

Step 6: Arrival & Local Reg.

+

Customs: Upon arrival, your pet will undergo a veterinary check and documents will be inspected. Customs fees/taxes may apply.

Local License: After entering the country (usually within 1-2 weeks), you must register your pet with the local municipality (e.g., Dubai Municipality, Abu Dhabi Municipality) and get an annual license (tag).

Temperature Restrictions

Restricted Travel Periods:

Most GCC countries restrict pet travel during summer months (typically May-September) when temperatures exceed safe limits. Airlines may refuse to transport pets when ground temperatures are above 29°C (85°F) or below 7°C (45°F).

Special Warning for Brachycephalic Breeds:

Short-nosed breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, Persian cats, etc.) are at higher risk in hot weather. Some airlines have additional restrictions or may refuse these breeds entirely during summer months.

Always Check:

Contact your airline directly before booking. Temperature restrictions vary by airline and can change daily based on weather forecasts at origin, destination, and transit airports.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a microchip and why is it mandatory?

+
A microchip is a permanent electronic ID tag (the size of a grain of rice) for your pet. It's mandatory in GCC countries for tracking lost/stolen pets, proof of ownership, and public health (rabies tracking). All vaccinations and tests must be done *after* this chip is implanted.

Is the Rabies Titer Test always required?

+
No, but YES for pets from "high-risk" countries (most of Europe, Turkey, USA, Canada, etc.). If you are coming from a "low-risk" (rabies-free) country (e.g., Australia, New Zealand, Japan, UK), it's usually not required. However, this list can change, check the MOCCAE list for the UAE.

Can my pet travel in the cabin with me?

+
Usually, no. Most GCC countries (especially UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia) only allow pets to enter as "manifested cargo." This means the animal must be collected from a special cargo terminal via specific procedures. Exceptions may exist (e.g., guide dogs), but for standard pets, cargo is the rule.

What is a restricted/banned breed?

+
All GCC countries prohibit the import and ownership of certain dog breeds (or their mixes) considered "dangerous." These lists vary by country but often include Pit Bull Terriers, Staffordshire Terriers, Tosa Inu, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, and some Mastiff types. Do not start the process without checking the country-specific list.

Are there age restrictions for importing pets?

+
Yes. Most GCC countries require pets to be at least 4 months old (some require 6 months) to allow for complete vaccination schedules. There is usually no maximum age limit, but very old pets may require additional health documentation. Puppies and kittens under the minimum age cannot be imported.

Can I bring multiple pets?

+
Limits vary by country. Saudi Arabia typically allows up to 2 pets per person. UAE and other countries may have different limits. Each pet requires separate permits, documents, and fees. Check the specific country's regulations before planning to import multiple pets.

Is quarantine required upon arrival?

+
Generally, no quarantine is required if all documents are in order and the pet arrives with proper permits. However, if documents are incomplete or there are health concerns, temporary quarantine may be enforced. Pets from certain countries or with incomplete vaccination records may face quarantine requirements.

How do I renew my pet's license after arrival?

+
After entering the country, you must register your pet with the local municipality (e.g., Dubai Municipality, Abu Dhabi Municipality) within 1-2 weeks. You'll receive an annual license tag. Renewal is required yearly, usually before the license expires. Keep vaccination records up to date for renewal. Fees vary by emirate/city.

Emergency Contacts

In case of emergency during pet travel:

Contact your airline's cargo department immediately. Have your pet's microchip number, flight details, and all documents ready. For veterinary emergencies at destination, contact local emergency veterinary clinics or the airport veterinary services.

Airport Veterinary Services:

Most GCC airports have 24/7 veterinary services. Contact information is usually available at the cargo terminal or through your airline.
